Where it started

It didn't start as a marketing idea. It started as an observation.

Living and working on the coast, I kept noticing the same thing: something shifted when people stepped out of an office and into the natural world. Walking side by side along the shoreline changed the rhythm of the conversation. People felt less guarded. Less stuck. More willing to be honest about what was actually going on.

The beach isn't replacing therapy. It's creating conditions that let therapy happen differently.

Why the beach works

Sometimes the environment matters.

Not magic — just a few things the setting does that an office can't.

Side by side, not across a desk

Walking next to someone takes the pressure off constant eye contact. For a lot of people, that's what finally makes the hard things easier to say.

The ocean creates perspective

The steady waves and the open horizon have a way of quieting the noise people carry in with them — so there's room to actually think.

Movement helps thinking

If you're someone who thinks better on your feet, sitting still in an office can get in the way. Out here, you don't have to.

What a session looks like

What to expect.

It's one of the only outdoor therapy options in Orange County — and it's more ordinary than it sounds.

  • We meet on the sand. You'll get a specific, easy-to-find spot ahead of time — close to parking, never a hike.
  • We walk and talk. An easy pace, no agenda to keep up with. The same depth as a session in the office.
  • Weather has a backup. If the coast isn't cooperating, we move to the Third Street office or meet by telehealth. You're never left stranded.
  • Privacy is taken seriously. We choose quiet times and stretches, keep a natural distance from others, and never use names within earshot. If it ever feels exposed, we head indoors.
  • Wear something comfortable. Good shoes and a layer. That's the whole list.

Common questions

Before you book.

Is Beach Therapy real therapy, or just a walk?
It's real therapy. Same training, same depth, and the same standards of care and privacy — we've changed the setting, not the work. For many people the walk is exactly what helps the work go deeper.
What if the weather's bad?
A gray morning never means a missed week. We keep an office session on Third Street or a telehealth visit as backup, and we'll sort out the plan with you ahead of time.
Is it private? It's a public beach.
We take privacy seriously. We choose quiet times and stretches of coast, keep a natural distance from other people, and never use names within earshot. If a session ever feels too exposed, we move indoors — your comfort comes first.
What should I wear or bring?
Comfortable shoes and a layer for the coastal breeze. Nothing else needed — no equipment, no prep.
Who is it a good fit for?
People who think better moving, or who feel boxed in by a traditional office, tend to take to it quickly. It isn't the right format for every concern or every session, and we'll be honest with you when indoors is the better call.
Does insurance cover Beach Therapy?
We're an out-of-network practice and provide a Superbill so you can seek reimbursement through your PPO plan. The fees page walks through exactly what to expect.
