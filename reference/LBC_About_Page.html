Why we exist

We didn't build a therapy factory. We built a practice.

I've always loved teaching. Academia gave me space to train therapists and think hard about what actually helps people change. But after enough years, I realized the work I cared about most wasn't happening in lecture halls — it was happening across from one person, in one room. So I built one.

What I wanted was a place where people didn't feel like cases, or diagnoses, or problems to be solved — but like people whose stories actually mattered.

We're not serving a market. We're serving our neighbors.

Our founder
Kay Wenger, LMFT, LPCC
Founder & Clinical Supervisor

For more than thirty years I've worked with individuals, couples, families, and teens — and spent three decades at Pepperdine training the next generation of therapists. I co-founded the National Registry of Marriage Friendly Therapists, chaired the MFT Consortium of Orange County, and founded the Heart to Heart program for older adults at our local Susi Q senior center.

My approach is solution-focused and integrative: I bring what I've learned across many theories and apply what actually fits the person in front of me. Most clients tell me I'm easy to talk to, and that I hold a lot of empathy for whatever they're carrying.

Techniques don't heal people. Relationships do.

How we work

What we believe about the work.

The same standard we hire and supervise to. It's less about a single method and more about how someone is met when they walk in.

Relationships heal — not techniques

Models and interventions matter, but none of them matter if the person across from you doesn't feel genuinely seen. We understand your experience before trying to change it.

Understood and challenged

Good therapy needs both — insight and movement. You should feel understood and helped toward a different way forward. Not just talking about your life, but changing it.

Curiosity over certainty

We stay curious about who you actually are rather than reaching for the nearest label. As Kay tells the therapists she trains: curiosity will take you farther than certainty.

Warm, and never grim

Hard topics don't have to feel heavy. There's room for ease — and the occasional smile — even in serious work. The goal isn't a perfect life; it's the resilience to handle a real one.

The team

A team Kay trained — and personally supervises.

Every associate holds an M.A. from Pepperdine or a comparable program, and each one's clinical work is supervised directly by Kay. When you reach out, we match you with the person who fits what you're navigating — not whoever's free.

Rooted in Laguna

We don't just know this community. We belong to it.

For more than twenty-five years, we've had the privilege of walking alongside people here through marriages and divorces, births and losses, career changes, caregiving, and retirement.

That's also why Kay founded Heart to Heart at the Susi Q senior center back in 2010 — a place for older adults to talk about what they're actually struggling with, not just stay busy. A local practice carries a different kind of accountability.
