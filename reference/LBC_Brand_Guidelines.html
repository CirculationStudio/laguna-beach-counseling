01
Visual Concept
"Navigating Life's Waves"

A coastal editorial system designed to make over thirty years of Pepperdine-trained clinical experience feel as warm and accessible as the practice already is in person. Not clinical. Not generic. Unmistakably Laguna.

Circulation Studio has developed the "Navigating Life's Waves" design system to give Laguna Beach Counseling a digital presence that finally matches the depth of what's actually happening inside the practice. The current TherapySites template doesn't reflect the warmth of Kay's voice, the gravity of her credentials, or the category-of-one nature of Beach Therapy. This system changes that.

The concept retains every brand asset Vanessa designed in the original identity — the lifeguard tower mark, the five-color palette, the "Navigating Life's Waves Together" tagline — and rebuilds the design system around them. We replace the unrefined original type pairing with a contemporary editorial serif and a humanist sans — DM Serif Display and Manrope. We codify spacing, components, and interaction patterns into a real system. We commit to a photography direction that bans every stock-therapy cliché.

Every choice is grounded in the strategic positioning documented in the Client DNA and Brand Voice Style Guide: premium out-of-network practice, warm-direct-real voice, audience that skews older and affluent. Type scales bumped 25% for readability. Buttons treated as pills, echoing the life ring in the logo. Gold reserved for exactly one CTA per screen — the page's main door.

This isn't theoretical. Every component shown here exists in the production homepage mockup. It's a buildable system designed to serve the practice for the next decade.

Coastal, not clinical

Sand-warm backgrounds, navy depth, sea foam softness. Every surface signals "you're in good hands, by the ocean" — never "you're in a waiting room."

Editorial gravitas

The serif does the emotional work — headlines, names, pull quotes, the tagline. The sans does the functional work. Never blur the line. The serif is the voice. The sans is the work.

Whitespace as credential

Confident practices don't crowd their pages. Templates do. Generous space says "we know what we're doing — we don't need to convince you with density."

Pills, not rectangles

Buttons and badges use 999px radius — they echo the life ring inside the logo. The whole system has three radii: pills, cards (16–22px), inputs (8px). That's it.

Accessibility & Approved Text Pairings

AA

Navy on Sand / Sand Warm. The primary reading combination. High contrast, comfortable for long copy and the older-skewing audience.

AA

White on Navy. Use for navy sections, the footer, and dark cards. Reverse the lockup to its white version here.

AA

Navy on Sun Gold. The only approved text color on a gold CTA — never white. Keeps the button legible and on-brand.

Target WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text) on every surface. Sun Gold and Sea Foam are accents, not text colors — never set body copy in either, and never place gold text on a light background.

03
Typography
DM Serif Display + Manrope.

The original Noto Serif / Manjari pairing rendered inconsistently and lacked editorial gravitas. The brand typeface system pairs DM Serif Display — a high-contrast editorial serif that carries every emotional moment — with Manrope, a geometric humanist sans that does all the functional work. Two families. No exceptions.

Serif · DM Serif DisplayHeadlines · Names · Pull Quotes · Tagline
There's a way through this.

DM Serif Display carries every emotional moment, with italics reserved for the single most loaded phrase per headline. Set it at 400 weight — its high contrast does the work; never bold it.

Sans · ManropeBody · UI · Buttons · Forms · Navigation
A geometric humanist sans with subtle warmth in its terminals. Confident and modern without feeling cold.

Weights 400, 500, 600, 700. Handles every functional touchpoint — body copy, form labels, button text, navigation. Sized at 18px base (was 16) to support the older-skewing audience.

04
Textures & Effects
Tactile depth without digital flatness.

Subtle effects that give the system architectural warmth — paper grain, soft foam, atmospheric depth. None of them are visible at first glance. All of them you'd miss if they weren't there.

Paper Grain Overlay
Sea Foam Wash
Pill Pattern Accent
Paper Grain

Inline SVG fractal noise at 4.5% opacity (multiply blend). Applied to sand-warm sections. Adds the tactile quality of letterpress paper. Absent on white and navy where it would muddy contrast.

Long, Soft Shadows

Three-layer composite: 1px crisp inner, 8px close diffuse, 24px ambient bloom. Cards float on the sand instead of sticking to it. Hover state pushes lift to -6px.

Layered Gold CTA

Solid gold base, inset highlight on top edge, embedded navy circle with arrow, gold-tinted shadow underneath. The CTA has dimension without using a 2010s gradient.

Scroll Reveal

Fade-in-up (translateY 28px) with staggered 120ms delays. Cubic-bezier easing. Each section reveals as you arrive at it — never all at once, never aggressively.

07
Tone of Voice
Warm. Direct. Real.

From the Brand Voice Style Guide: warmth 9.7/10, directness 9.6/10, humor and lightness 10/10, formality 4/10. The voice sounds like Kay — not like a brand or a brochure.

01

Deeply Warm

9.7 / 10

Warmth is the defining characteristic — the natural texture of how Kay engages with people. It shows up in speaking directly to someone's experience, normalizing what they're going through, and describing the work plainly.

02

Genuinely Direct

9.6 / 10 directness · 4 / 10 formality

Low formality paired with high directness. Says what it means. Doesn't dress things up in clinical language. "Here's what I'm actually seeing" — and have it land as care, not criticism.

03

Light but Never Lightweight

10 / 10 humor & lightness

Not comedy. Not deflecting from hard things. Ease. Room for a knowing observation, a moment that acknowledges how genuinely complicated life gets, a smile tucked inside a sentence. What Esther Perel does.

Copy Do's & Don'ts — from the Style Guide

✓ This sounds like you
"You're here because something needs to shift — and you're probably not sure exactly where to start. Maybe it's your relationship, or the anxiety that won't quiet down, or a transition you didn't see coming."
✗ Too clinical and cold
"Our evidence-based therapeutic interventions utilize a multidisciplinary framework to optimize client outcomes across a spectrum of presenting concerns."
✓ This sounds like you
"I bring empathy, directness, and yes, occasionally a sense of humor. Because sometimes the most human thing you can do is acknowledge how complicated life gets — and maybe smile a little at it."
✗ Hype-y and sales-driven
"5 breakthrough strategies to transform your relationship! Unlock your potential this week and finally be your best self."
✓ This sounds like you
"Most people who reach out for therapy have already tried a few things that didn't work. They've read the books, maybe talked to a friend, maybe even tried therapy before and it didn't quite click. That's not a failure — that's just what it looks like when you're trying to find the right fit."
✗ Too heavy / grim
"Life is filled with suffering and pain. Anxiety, depression, and relationship failure affect millions. But there may be hope — if you're truly ready to do the hard work."
Voice in action · Instagram post

Anxiety has a way of convincing you that the worst-case scenario is also the most likely one.

It's not that you're being irrational. It's that your brain is doing exactly what it was designed to do — scan for threats, prepare for the worst, keep you safe. The problem is it can't always tell the difference between a real emergency and an uncomfortable conversation.

That's not a character flaw. That's just how the nervous system works.

Understanding that distinction doesn't make anxiety disappear. But it does give you something to work with.

That's usually where we start.

Usage Rules

Minimum Clear Space

Always maintain clear space equal to the lifeguard tower's height on all sides. Never crowd the mark with adjacent elements or photography overlap.

Approved Backgrounds

Square emblem on Sand, Navy, Sea Foam, or Sun Gold. Horizontal lockup on Sand or Navy. Never on busy photography without an overlay. Never recolored.

Logo Misuse — Never Do This

✕

Don't recolor the tower or any part of the mark. The palette is fixed.

✕

Don't stretch, squash, rotate, or otherwise distort the proportions.

✕

Don't place the mark on busy photography without a legibility overlay.

✕

Don't pair the lockup with non-brand typography or add a drop shadow.

✕

Don't crop the lockup, box it in, or violate the clear-space rule.

✕

Don't re-typeset the tagline — use the supplied lockup artwork only.
